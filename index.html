Prompt templates by question type

The four prompts below are lifted verbatim from SpatialScore's official evaluation script (test_qwen.py). Each sample is wrapped in one of these according to its question_type — with the metric variant of open-ended applied whenever extra_info carries answer_value/answer_unit. For multi-choice, options are auto-formatted as (A) opt1 / (B) opt2 / ... and appended to the question.

judgement · Yes/No
judgement
Please answer with yes or no based on the image. Respond ONLY with 'yes' or 'no'. Question: {question}
multi-choice · A/B/C/D
multi-choice
Please select the most appropriate answer from the given options. Respond ONLY with the capital letter and its parentheses. Question: {question} (A) ... (B) ... (C) ... (D) ...
open-ended · generic
open-ended (scalar)
Please answer the question based on the given image or video. Respond ONLY with a concise and accurate scalar or a scalar with corresponding unit. Question: {question}
open-ended · metric
open-ended (3D distance)
Please answer the question by measuring the precise distance in 3D space through 2D images or videos. Respond ONLY with a numeric answer consisting of a scalar and a distance unit in the format of \scalar{scalar} \distance_unit{distance unit}. Question: {question}

※ Scoring proceeds in two stages. (i) rule-based — exact letter match for multi-choice, yes/no match for judgement, VSI-Bench MRA (Mean Relative Accuracy, threshold 0.5–0.95, step 0.05) for open-ended; (ii) LLM-based (GPT-OSS-20B) — answer extraction fallback for the cases where the rule fails.

Spatial Map
id 3372
multi-choice single-image Mental Animation SpatialEval
How many objects are in the East of Yacht Club?
A synthetic 2D map shows several named objects (Yacht Club, etc.) placed at random points. Every sample fills two slots — a direction and an anchor object — into the template "How many objects are in the [direction] of [anchor]?". SpatialEval discretises the pairwise angle between objects into eight compass wedges (N, NE, E, SE, S, SW, W, NW), but the generator never places two objects on the same row or column. As a result, ALL pairwise relations land in the four diagonal wedges (NE / NW / SE / SW), and any counting question with a cardinal direction (E / W / N / S) is answered 0 by construction — confirmed across all 32 such samples in the benchmark. So even though objects clearly sit to the right of Yacht Club in the picture, those are NE or SE relative to it, not E. The intended answer is 0 regardless of the visible layout.
  • (A)5
  • (B)4
  • (C)3
  • (D)0
answerD
